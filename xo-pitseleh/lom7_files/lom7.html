Item Search


Notes Before Starting:
-This chart lets you know where to look for any item in the game. Except rare weapons and armor...
-Items in red are those that are limitedd to a certain amount per game.
-Further down the page is a chart that eexplains how to get the different level of shops like "Item Shop 1" and so on.
-Some people might think the Polter Box pet increases your chances of getting better rare items. This is only about half-true. All monsters have 8 possible levels of treasure they can drop. What the Polter Box's Synchro does is prevent monsters from dropping their two most common treasures (Level 1 and Level 2 treasures). This ensures that you will always get either a treasure bag or candy since all monsters drop treasure bags or candy as their Level 3-8 treasures . The only exception is the Chess Knight which has Lucre and Jems as its Level 3 treasure. So the Polterbox doesn't necessarily increase your chances of getting better items from treasure bags, it just increases your chances of getting a treasure bag. Your odds of getting a Level 7 or 8 item are still about 1 in 32

-Monster Level 1 ~ 15 = Treasures Level 1 ~ 4
-Monster Level 16 ~ 31 = Treasures Levell 1 ~ 5
-Monster Level 32 ~ 47 = Treasures Levell 1 ~ 6
-Monster Level 48 ~ 63 = Treasures Levell 1 ~ 7
-Monster Level 64 ~ 99 = Treasures Levell 1 ~ 8

-The "Luck" of your hero and ppet seems to be a factor in determining what item you get from an enemy. So just because you're fighting a level 32 enemy doesn't mean you can't get an item the enemy drops at level 64 and up. But if the enemy is at like, level 8, don't expect to get a level 64 item from it.
-Some items can be obtained through playying the Shadow Zero mini-game in Domina on Salamander Day after completing the event "Tear Drop Crystal". However, I don't recommend getting your items this way unless you're truly desparate, masochistic, or both.

Land Strength & Shop Rank


Notes Before Starting:
-This chart probably looks confusing--and it is. But if you stare at it long enough you'll probably start to get it.

1) Under "Land Strength" you see there are 27 levels. A land will get 1 point for each artifact already placed on the map and 1 point for every space it is placed away from "My Home". The levels start at 3 because My Home (1 point) + Domina (1 point) + One space away from My Home (1 point) = 3. So Domina always has a Land Strength of 3. If I were to place "Mekiv Caverns" next to Domina then the Land Strength of Mekiv Caverns would be: My Home (1 point) + Domina (1 point) + Mekiv Caverns (1 point) + Two spaces away from My Home (2 points) = 5. So Mekiv Caverns would have a Land Strength of 5 if you placed it like that. Basically, to determine the Land Strength of an artifact you're about to place you: count the number of lands already on your map, count the number of spaces the land will be from My Home, and add 1 point for the artifact you're about to place.

2) Shops sell different items depending on what your Land Strength is. For example: looking at the chart you can see that if Gato Grattoes has a Land Strength of 5 through 9 then the Equipment Shop in Gato Grattoes will be a Rank 1 Equipment Shop and sell only stuff that a Rank 1 Equipment Shop would sell. (The next chart shows what each type of shop will sell depending on its Rank.)
