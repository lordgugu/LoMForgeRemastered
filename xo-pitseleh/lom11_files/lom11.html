Notes Before Starting:
-I've provided a column for the best maaterial-instrument-coin combination. There are certainly others that will work but I assume that people are only interested in the combination that gives the highest attack power for that spell. Besides, the list for all material-instrument-coin combinations is gigantic and there is no way I'm translating that.
-In cases where the best combination reequires a very rare material, I've also given an alternate combination. The alternate is the strongest combination that doesn't use a super rare material such as Dior Wood, Dragon's Scale Fossil, Gaius's Tear, Altena Alloy etc.) It's not necessarily the second best but a lot of times it is.
-Spells are distinguished by their &quoot;Area Effect", "Power", or "Bonus Effect". Notice that all spells that have the Area Effect of "Circle Fire" also have "C" Power and cause some sort of bad status effect to the enemy. This is the case with any spell with an Area Effect of "Circle Fire" no matter what the element.
-I wish direct translations for "MMagic Names" had've been used in the U.S version. Had this been done, you would've been able to distinguish a magic's effect's just by knowing it's name. In other words, if a direct translation had've been done, Wisp magics that started with the word "Saint" (Saint Flame, Saint Strike, Saint Burst, Saint Flare) would all have "A" level power, Wisp magics that began with the word "Holy" (Holy Slash, Holy Saber, Holy Stake, Holy Blast) would all have "B" level power and cause "Charm Down" to enemies etc. But with the way things were translated, it seems that there is no pattern to the Magic Names other than what the translator thought would sound cool.
-Since I had to comb over a list of aboout 3,400 different combinations to find the Best Instruments you can imagine that I may have made a mistake or two. Feel free to let me know where I've goofed.
